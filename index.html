Editing is something divine. Every serious written work requires its touch. A careful caress, gentle, yet penetrating, kind, but not forgiving; a beautiful healing process that transforms a butterfly into an eagle. Why on earth would anyone not want to be an editor? Perhaps they don't realize how awesome it is. But I do. And I sincerely hope that you will give me a shelter in your castle, where I can collect butterflies and turn them into…dragons.

My specialties include structural editing, developmental editing, stylistic editing, copyediting, and acquisitions.

Why hire me?


Short answer: I'm a lover of stories.

Yes, I know I keep saying this, but it's just so true! I guess when you spend enough time on something, it has to mean you love it. Because how long do you do something you don't like? A week? A month? Me, not even ten minutes. But I can easily do story stuff for a lifetime.

  •     I know how stories work
    Not only am I a writer myself but I also review hundreds of stories every year for my small publishing house. So I'm constantly analyzing the fundamentals of storytelling. I can tell if your pace is good, if you need more showing or telling, if your style is flowing or constricted, if your characters are not growing or acting inconsistently, and if your plot is logically flawed. And I know how to fix these problems.
  •     I have the required language skills
    I'm not native. Concerned about this?
    Look at this from my perspective. I'm not native, so ever since childhood, I was always worried about my language skills. It was this diffidence that pushed me to master the language. Because I'm not native, I need to question every small thing to ensure it's correct. Today my language is not distinct from a native person's, but this habit has become second nature. It also happens to be a key ability every editor should have. And if you're not sold on my philosophy, let me prove my skills to you in my free editing sample : )
  •     I use the industry-recommended resources
    Editorial wisdom and practice over the decades has been compiled in books called style guides. There is no absolute resource for fiction, but I'm fully capable of handling any fiction issue. If there's a tricky language issue, I refer to a style guide. I use The Chicago Manual of Style, 17th Edition and The Copyeditor's Handbook by Amy Einsohn and Marilyn Schwartz. I follow Merriam-Webster for spelling.
    Please note that style guides are mainly used for non-fiction. Fiction editors do not follow every rule or preference. As such, the standard rules are always respected and the preferences can be substituted with those of the author's.
  •     I'm always evolving
    I'm sufficiently knowledgeable and skilled to be worthy of your trust. I do not need a certificate to validate my skills—the free editing sample should tell you all you need to know. However, I also believe that the ladder of learning never ends. I'm currently enrolled in a certificate course in advanced editing offered by Queen's University, Canada. I just scored 100% in Structural Editing Standards!
  •     I want you to evolve as well
    Most of my clients are returning clients. I don't mind investing my time in answering your questions if this helps me in editing your second manuscript a bit faster. But this is not just about business. If I sign up with you, it also means I like you—I never work with people I dislike. If I like you, we will become friends. And I'm just an awesome guy who wants the best for his friends : )
